## I Baked a Red Velvet Cake Because I Felt Like It.

There are evenings where you doom scroll and call it a day. And then there are evenings where you decide, for no real reason, that you’re going to bake a full red velvet cake.

This was that kind of evening.

![IMG_1450.jpeg](/blog/a-red-velvet-cake-recipe-also/IMG_1450.jpeg)

---

## The Setup

I didn’t plan it. I just wanted red velvet. Not chocolate, not vanilla. Red velvet. Slightly extra, a little dramatic, but worth it.

So I pulled everything out. Butter, eggs, buttermilk. Let it all sit out so it behaves properly, like I actually know what I’m doing.

![IMG_1451.jpeg](/blog/a-red-velvet-cake-recipe-also/IMG_1451.jpeg)

---

## The Batter

I started with the dry ingredients. Flour, a bit of cocoa, baking soda, salt. Mixed and kept aside.

In another bowl, butter and sugar got beaten until smooth. Eggs went in one at a time. Then oil, vinegar, vanilla, and buttermilk.

It looked a bit odd at this stage, not going to lie. But that’s normal.

Dry ingredients went in slowly, mixed until everything came together. Then the red food coloring.

Not a hint of red. Proper red.

![IMG_1452.jpeg](/blog/a-red-velvet-cake-recipe-also/IMG_1452.jpeg)

---

## Into the Oven

Batter into two pans, oven already at 175°C.

![85D04D02-DBE8-47A3-942A-F478CC675145.jpeg](/blog/a-red-velvet-cake-recipe-also/85D04D02-DBE8-47A3-942A-F478CC675145.jpeg)

It baked for about 35–40 minutes. I checked on it more often than necessary, obviously.

Toothpick came out clean, so that was enough reassurance.

---

## Cooling

Let the cakes sit for a bit, then turned them out and left them to cool completely.

![IMG_1462.jpeg](/blog/a-red-velvet-cake-recipe-also/IMG_1462.jpeg)

This part requires patience, which I don’t naturally have.

---

## The Frosting

Cream cheese, butter, powdered sugar, vanilla, and a pinch of salt.

Beat until smooth and fluffy. Tasted it a couple of times just to be sure.

![IMG_1456.jpeg](/blog/a-red-velvet-cake-recipe-also/IMG_1456.jpeg)

Oh it was too good, I can't lie.

![IMG_1460.jpeg](/blog/a-red-velvet-cake-recipe-also/IMG_1460.jpeg)

---

## Assembly

Leveled the cake layers so they sit flat.

![IMG_1466.jpeg](/blog/a-red-velvet-cake-recipe-also/IMG_1466.jpeg)

Frosting on the first layer, second layer on top, then covered the whole thing. Not perfectly neat, but good enough.

Used the leftover cake scraps, baked them briefly to dry them out, then turned them into crumbs and pressed them onto the sides.

![IMG_1467.jpeg](/blog/a-red-velvet-cake-recipe-also/IMG_1467.jpeg)

## The Result

A little messy, but solid.

Soft, rich, slightly tangy from the frosting, and very red.

## RECIPE:

<b>For Cake</br>:
- [ ] ½ cup approx. 115g butter, room temperature
- [ ] 1-2 tablespoons Red food coloring
- [ ] 2 teaspoons Vanilla extract
- [ ] 2 Eggs
- [ ] 1 teaspoon white Vinegar
- [ ] 2 tbsp Vegetable oil
- [ ] 1 cup approx. 240ml buttermilk, room temperature
- [ ] 1 teaspoon Salt
- [ ] 1½ cups apprx. 300g Sugar
- [ ] 1 teaspoon Baking soda
- [ ] 2 tablespoons approx. 16g Cocoa powder
- [ ] 2½ cups approx. 310g all-purpose flour

<b>For the Cream Cheese Frosting:</b>
- [ ] cream cheese room temperature (450g)
- [ ] 1 ½ cups unsalted butter room temperature (approx 340g)
- [ ] 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- [ ] Pinch of salt
- [ ] Confectioners sugar sifted (5½ cups/675g)

<b>Method</b>

1. Preheat oven to 350F (175C). 

2. In a large bowl sift flour, cocoa powder, baking soda and salt. Stir and set aside. 

3. In a separate large bowl, beat butter and sugar until smooth. Add eggs one at the time, beating until combined after each addition. Add oil, vinegar, vanilla extract, buttermilk and beat until combined. Gradually add the dry ingredients and mix after each addition until well combined. Add food coloring and mix. 

4. Pour the batter into two 8 inch baking trays greased with butter and dust with flour. or you can use one pan and bake twice. Also you can use oven pan lined with parchment. 

5. Bake 35-40 minutes, until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

6. Cool the cakes in their pans on a wire rack for 10 minutes. Release from the pan, then let cool completely. 

7. Make the frosting: in a large bowl, beat cream cheese with powdered sugar and vanilla extract. Beat until smooth and creamy. In a separate bowl with heavy cream to stiff peaks. Then fold into the cream cheese mixture.

8. Cut the top of the cakes. Cut 8-12 heart shapes from the top layer of the cakes. Make crumbs from the leftovers. Set aside. 

9. Place one cake layer with flat side down. Spread a layer of frosting, place the second layer of cake on top of the frosting, flat side up. Spread evenly the frosting on top and sides of the cake.

10. make the crumbs using the leftover cake (the discarded portions?) by baking them again at 200C for like 5 mins -10 mins andt they should come out dry almost crumbly
