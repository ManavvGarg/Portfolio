## I Baked a Red Velvet Cake Because I Felt Like It.

There are evenings where you doom scroll and call it a day. And then there are evenings where you decide, for no real reason, that you’re going to bake a full red velvet cake.

This was that kind of evening.

---

## The Setup

I didn’t plan it. I just wanted red velvet. Not chocolate, not vanilla. Red velvet. Slightly extra, a little dramatic, but worth it.

So I pulled everything out. Butter, eggs, buttermilk. Let it all sit out so it behaves properly, like I actually know what I’m doing.

---

## The Batter

I started with the dry ingredients. Flour, a bit of cocoa, baking soda, salt. Mixed and kept aside.

In another bowl, butter and sugar got beaten until smooth. Eggs went in one at a time. Then oil, vinegar, vanilla, and buttermilk.

It looked a bit odd at this stage, not going to lie. But that’s normal.

Dry ingredients went in slowly, mixed until everything came together. Then the red food coloring.

Not a hint of red. Proper red.

---

## Into the Oven

Batter into two pans, oven already at 175°C.

It baked for about 35–40 minutes. I checked on it more often than necessary, obviously.

Toothpick came out clean, so that was enough reassurance.

---

## Cooling

Let the cakes sit for a bit, then turned them out and left them to cool completely.

This part requires patience, which I don’t naturally have.

---

## The Frosting

Cream cheese, butter, powdered sugar, vanilla, and a pinch of salt.

Beat until smooth and fluffy. Tasted it a couple of times just to be sure.

---

## Assembly

Leveled the cake layers so they sit flat.

Frosting on the first layer, second layer on top, then covered the whole thing. Not perfectly neat, but good enough.

Used the leftover cake scraps, baked them briefly to dry them out, then turned them into crumbs and pressed them onto the sides.

---

## The Result

A little messy, but solid.

Soft, rich, slightly tangy from the frosting, and very red.

---

## Final Thought

It’s a bit unnecessary to bake a full cake on a random evening.

But sometimes that’s exactly the point.
